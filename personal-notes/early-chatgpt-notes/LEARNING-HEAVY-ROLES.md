# Learning Heavy Roles

## Overview

The jobs with the most learning usually involve synthesizing knowledge and explaining it to others.

That’s why:

- researchers
- consultants
- educators
- investors

learn the most.

## Roles

1. Research-Oriented Roles

These roles spend a large percentage of time reading, experimenting, and understanding new domains.

Examples

- AI Research Engineer
- Applied Scientist
- Research Software Engineer
- R&D Architect

Typical work

- Read research papers
- Prototype ideas
- Evaluate emerging technology
- Write internal reports

Good companies

- OpenAI
- Anthropic
- DeepMind
- Microsoft Research

Learning fraction: often 40–60%.


2. Developer Advocate / Technical Evangelist

This role is basically “professional learner + explainer.”

What you do:

- Learn new technology early
- Build demos
- Write tutorials
- Speak at conferences

Companies that hire these:

- Google
- Stripe
- Cloudflare
- HashiCorp

Learning fraction: 30–50%.


3. Venture Capital / Technical Due Diligence

Many VCs hire senior engineers to evaluate startups and technologies.

Typical work

- Analyze emerging tech companies
- Meet founders
- Evaluate markets
- Write memos

You end up learning about:

- robotics
- biotech
- AI
- energy
- global economics

Examples

- Andreessen Horowitz
- Sequoia Capital
- Y Combinator

Learning fraction: 50%+.


4. Strategy / Innovation Roles

These roles exist inside large companies.

Titles

- Innovation Architect
- Emerging Technology Strategist
- Future-of-Technology Lead
- CTO Office Architect

Typical work

- Track emerging tech
- Evaluate new tools
- run proofs-of-concept
- advise executives

Companies with these roles:

- Amazon
- IBM
- Accenture

Learning fraction: 30–50%.


5. Consultant / Fractional Architect

Consultants constantly learn because every client has a different domain.

One month you might learn:

- healthcare systems
- robotics manufacturing
- fintech regulation
- AI infrastructure

Typical companies

- McKinsey & Company
- Boston Consulting Group
- Thoughtworks

Learning fraction: 30–60%.


6. Technical Writer / Educator

You might like this given your broad interests in languages, religion, philosophy, etc.

Examples

- Author of technical books
- Course creator
- Educational platform engineer

Platforms include:

- Brilliant
- Khan Academy
- Coursera

Learning fraction: often 50%+.


7. Intelligence / Analysis Roles

These jobs literally exist to learn about the world.

Examples:

- geopolitical analyst
- economic intelligence analyst
- technology intelligence analyst

Organizations:

- RAND Corporation
- Council on Foreign Relations

Learning fraction: extremely high.


8. Indie Builder / Explorer (Underrated Option)

Many senior engineers end up doing something like:

- running a YouTube channel
- writing a newsletter
- building experimental apps
- teaching courses

Examples:

- Substack
- YouTube

Your job becomes “learn interesting things and publish them.”


## Roles that might fit me best

Based on interests: AI, languages, history, games, philosophy, etc.:

- AI Research Engineer
- Developer Advocate
- Technical VC Analyst
- Innovation Architect
- Educational Platform Builder

All of these allow learning as a core activity rather than a side activity.
