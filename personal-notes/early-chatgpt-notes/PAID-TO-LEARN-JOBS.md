# Unusual jobs where you get paid to learn

## Overview

Here are 10 unusual jobs where learning is literally the core activity. Many people don’t realize these roles exist, but they often attract people who are naturally curious about many subjects — which seems very aligned with your interests (AI, languages, philosophy, history, games, etc.).

## Jobs

1. Futurist

A futurist studies trends and predicts how technology and society may evolve.

What you do

- Study emerging technologies
- Analyze global trends
- Model possible futures
- Advise companies or governments

Companies and institutions hire them to answer questions like:

- What will AI do to jobs?
- What industries will exist in 20 years?

Example organization:

- Institute for the Future

Learning fraction: ~70%


2. Technology Scout

Large companies hire scouts to discover emerging technologies.

Typical work

- Read research papers
- Talk to startups
- Attend conferences
- identify promising innovations

Companies that use scouts:

- Intel
- Samsung
- Procter & Gamble

Your job is literally: “find things nobody knows about yet.”


3. Think Tank Researcher

Think tanks exist almost entirely to study complex issues.

Topics include:

- geopolitics
- AI policy
- economics
- global conflicts

Organizations include:

- Brookings Institution
- RAND Corporation
- Carnegie Endowment for International Peace

Your job is reading, research, and publishing reports.


4. Intelligence Analyst

These roles analyze world events and emerging risks.

Topics studied:

- military conflicts
- economics
- cyber warfare
- energy markets

Organizations include:

- Central Intelligence Agency
- National Security Agency

Many analysts spend most of their time learning and synthesizing information.


5. Museum Curator

Curators are experts who study a field and build exhibits.

Fields include:

- ancient history
- religion
- languages
- anthropology
- science

Institutions include:

- Smithsonian Institution

Much of the job involves research and discovery.


6. Game Narrative Designer

This is the role that builds the story and lore of games.

To do it well, designers often study:

- mythology
- history
- philosophy
- linguistics

Companies include:

- FromSoftware
- Nintendo

If you enjoyed games like Dark Souls, the lore behind them comes from roles like this.


7. Academic Generalist (Interdisciplinary Scholar)

Some academics intentionally work across multiple disciplines.

Examples:

- history of science
- cognitive science
- religious studies
- comparative linguistics

Universities like:

- Massachusetts Institute of Technology
- Stanford University

Learning and writing papers is the primary activity.


8. Professional Explainer (Author / Course Creator)

Some people make careers by learning something and explaining it well.

Examples:

- science communicators
- technical authors
- educational video creators

Platforms include:

- YouTube
- Substack
- Brilliant

This is essentially the job:

Learn fascinating things and explain them clearly.


9. Open-Source Explorer

Some engineers specialize in experimenting with new technology and publishing what they learn.

Typical activities:

- testing new frameworks
- benchmarking tools
- writing tutorials
- building prototypes

Companies sometimes hire this role under titles like:

- “Principal Engineer — Emerging Technology”
- “Developer Experience Architect”


10. Polymath / Independent Scholar

A small number of people structure their careers around wide curiosity across fields.

They may:

- write books
- give talks
- consult
- advise startups

Examples include people like:

- Tim Urban
- Yuval Noah Harari

Their work is essentially curiosity-driven research.


## The Pattern

The highest-learning jobs tend to fall into four archetypes:

Researcher - Discover new knowledge
Scout - Discover new technologies
Analyst - Understand complex systems
Explainer - Teach others what they learned


## Roles for me

Based on my interest in topics like:
AI, languages, philosophy, religion, history, games, world economics

The roles that seem most aligned with me are:

- Innovation / Emerging Tech Architect
- AI Research Engineer
- Developer Advocate
- Technology Futurist
- Educational Platform Builder

Those roles reward wide curiosity rather than narrow specialization.
