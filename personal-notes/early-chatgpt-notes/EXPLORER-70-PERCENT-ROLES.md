# Careers That Are ~70% Explorer

1. Technology Scout / Emerging Tech Analyst

This role exists inside large companies, venture firms, and research groups.

What you do

- monitor emerging technologies
- read research papers
- talk with startups
- experiment with new tools
- brief leadership

You might study in one month:

- robotics
- battery chemistry
- AI models
- satellite networks
- biotech

Organizations that do this:

- Intel
- Samsung
- Google

Explorer fraction: ~70–80%


2. Venture Capital Technical Partner

Many venture capital firms hire technical experts to evaluate startups.

Typical work

- meet founders
- explore new technologies
- analyze markets
- write investment memos

Every startup introduces you to a new industry.

Examples:

- Andreessen Horowitz
- Sequoia Capital
- Y Combinator

Explorer fraction: ~70%


3. Futurist / Strategic Foresight Researcher

These roles focus on understanding long-term societal and technological change.

Typical activities:

- track emerging trends
- read academic research
- interview experts
- build future scenarios

Organizations:

- Institute for the Future
- World Economic Forum

Explorer fraction: ~80%


4. Think Tank Researcher

Think tanks constantly explore new global issues.

Possible topics:

- AI governance
- global conflicts
- economic systems
- climate technologies
- cyber warfare

Institutions:

- RAND Corporation
- Brookings Institution
- Explorer fraction: ~70–80%


5. Technology Journalist

Good journalists must constantly learn new fields quickly.

Typical work:

- interview experts
- research emerging topics
- explain developments to readers

Publications:

- MIT Technology Review
- The Economist

Explorer fraction: ~75%


6. Independent Researcher / Author

Some people essentially build careers around curiosity-driven exploration.

Activities might include:

- writing books
- producing documentaries
- giving talks
- publishing essays

Platforms:

- Substack
- YouTube

Explorer fraction: nearly unlimited

## A Less Obvious Role That Might Fit Me Very Well

“Emerging Technology Architect”

This role exists at some companies but goes by many names:

- Principal Technologist
- CTO Office Architect
- Emerging Technology Architect
- Emerging Technology Lead
- Innovation Architect

What these people do:

- explore new technologies
- prototype ideas
- evaluate trends
- advise leadership

Companies that often have roles like this:

- Amazon
- Microsoft
- IBM

Explorer fraction: ~60–70%


## NOT Explorer-High Roles

Staff Engineer = Often execution-heavy
Engineering Manager = Mostly coordination
Startup Founder = Mostly operations
Product Manager = Mostly meetings


## Possible best for me

Given my background and interests, the three roles closest to my ideal mix are likely:

1. Technology Scout / Emerging Tech Analyst
2. Venture Capital Technical Partner
3. Futurist / Strategic Foresight Researcher

All three involve constantly exploring new domains.


## Happy Work

People who are Explorer-dominant often end up happiest when they structure their career around three activities:

1. Scanning the frontier (new ideas)
2. Testing the frontier (experiments)
3. Explaining the frontier (briefings or writing)

When a job includes all three, the work can feel endlessly interesting.
