# Polymnath Career Path #1

## Overview

There is one career path that unusually combines technology, geopolitics, culture, economics, languages, and philosophy, and it tends to be extremely exploration-heavy.

That path is often called:

`Technology Geopolitics` / `Tech Intelligence Analyst`

This field studies how technology shapes global power.

Instead of just building technology, people in this role analyze questions like:

- How will AI change global military power?
- How will semiconductors shape geopolitics?
- How will social media affect political stability?
- Which countries will dominate robotics, energy, or space?

These analysts constantly explore technology + global systems.

## What the Job Actually Looks Like

A typical week might include:

1. Research

- reading academic papers
- studying economic reports
- analyzing new technologies

2. Monitoring

- tracking new AI models
- watching geopolitical developments
- following startup ecosystems worldwide

3. Synthesis

- writing briefings
- presenting insights
- advising leadership


## Who Employs These Analysts

### Policy Think Tanks

Organizations studying global strategy

Examples:

- RAND Corporation
- Center for Strategic and International Studies
- Brookings Institution

### Intelligence Agencies

These roles analyze global technology competition.

Examples:

- Central Intelligence Agency
- National Security Agency

### Big Tech Strategy Groups

Large technology companies also hire people to study geopolitical tech competition.

Examples:

- Microsoft
- Google

## Why This Role Is Explorer-Dominant

The work requires learning about many fields simultaneously:

| Domain | Why it matters |
| ------ | -------------- |
| AI | strategic competition |
| Semiconductors | global supply chains |
| Economics | industrial policy |
| Languages | international research |
| History | geopolitical context |
| Culture | societal adoption of technology |

People in this field spend a huge amount of time learning new things.

Explorer fraction: 70–80%

## Example Questions These Analysts Study

Some real questions studied in this field include:

- Will AI change nuclear deterrence?
- Which countries will dominate quantum computing?
- Could robotics reshape global manufacturing?
- How will climate technology shift geopolitics?

This is basically technology + world history unfolding in real time.
