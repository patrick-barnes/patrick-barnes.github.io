# Explorer-heavy industries

## Overview

There are a few industries where exploration is actually part of the business model. These sectors employ a disproportionate number of people whose job is to scan the frontier of ideas rather than just execute.

Below are three industries that employ a surprisingly large number of Explorer-type roles.

## Industries

1. Venture Capital & Startup Investment

This is probably the most exploration-heavy industry outside academia.

VC firms constantly explore:

- new technologies
- new markets
- new scientific discoveries
- new business models

Technical experts are often hired as partners, advisors, or analysts to evaluate emerging technologies.

Typical roles:

- Technical Partner
- Venture Partner
- Deep Tech Analyst
- Startup Scout

Examples of firms:

- Andreessen Horowitz
- Sequoia Capital
- Y Combinator

Why explorers thrive here:

- every meeting exposes you to a new industry
- you are rewarded for curiosity
- pattern recognition across fields is valuable

Explorer ratio: extremely high


2. Large Tech “Future Strategy” Groups

Many big tech companies have internal groups focused on emerging technology.

These teams exist to answer questions like:

- What will AI do in 10 years?
- What new computing platforms might appear?
- What technologies threaten our business?

Typical groups include:

- CTO Office
- Innovation Lab
- Advanced Technology Group

Companies with these teams:

- Google
- Microsoft
- IBM

Roles include:

- Emerging Technology Architect
- Innovation Strategist
- Technology Futures Lead

Explorer ratio: high


3. Think Tanks & Policy Research

This industry hires people specifically to understand complex systems.

Topics explored:

- global economics
- military strategy
- AI governance
- energy transitions
- geopolitics

Organizations include:

- RAND Corporation
- Brookings Institution
- Center for Strategic and International Studies

Explorer ratio: very high


4. Intelligence & Risk Analysis (bonus industry, often overlooked)

These organizations constantly study emerging threats and global systems.

Analysts may explore:

- cyber warfare
- geopolitics
- energy markets
- emerging technologies

Organizations include:

- Central Intelligence Agency
- National Security Agency

Explorer ratio: very high


## Pattern

Explorer-heavy jobs exist where the organization needs to answer:

“What’s coming next?”

Industries that depend on that question include:

| Industry | Why exploration matters |
| -------- | ----------------------- |
| Venture capital | finding future companies |
| Big tech strategy | anticipating disruption |
| Think tanks | understanding global systems |
| Intelligence | predicting threats |
